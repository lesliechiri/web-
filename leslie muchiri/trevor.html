ROLE OF POLICE IN SOUTH AFRICA TODAY

The role of police in the current South African era, like many police forces, is to maintain law and order. Since South Africa is a rainbow nation it accommodates both white and black without discrimination therefore respecting diversity. They also control traffic law enforcement on local roads to avoid congestion of vehicles. They also deal with protest action to make sure they are peaceful demonstrations to avoid instances of violence and looting leading to damage of property and loss of lives.

The force consists of the Metro police and the South African police force. The metro police work locally focusing mainly on the duties named above and the South African police force works more with the investigative part.

The metro police account to the municipal government and are paid by the same whereas the police service account to the national ministry and are paid the national treasury.South Africa is a rainbow nation now therefore the force consists of both black and white.
